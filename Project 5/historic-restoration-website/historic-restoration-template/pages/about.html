We don't just restore buildings—we preserve the stories etched into every stone, timber, and mortar joint. At HeritageCraft, our mission is to protect the irreplaceable for generations to come.

Our Mission & Philosophy

Founded by master craftsman Edmund Hargrove in 1985, HeritageCraft was born from a singular belief: that historic structures deserve the same reverence as the history they hold. Every crack, every patina, every worn threshold tells a story that modern construction cannot replicate.

We blend rigorous historical research with state-of-the-art preservation science. Our team of certified architects, master masons, woodworkers, and conservators approach each project as both a technical challenge and a cultural responsibility.

Our Journey

Four decades of preserving America's architectural soul

1985
1985 Foundation Year

HeritageCraft Founded

Edmund Hargrove establishes HeritageCraft from a small workshop in Boston's historic district with a single crew of three, restoring a 19th-century brownstone row house.

1985
1991
1991
1991 Milestone

First National Historic Landmark

Awarded the contract to restore the Whitmore Federal Building — our first National Historic Landmark project. This cemented our reputation for working on the nation's most sensitive heritage structures.

1998
1998 Award

National Preservation Honor Award

Received the National Trust for Historic Preservation's Honor Award for Excellence in Craftsmanship, recognizing our restoration of the 1874 St. Augustine Cathedral's bell tower and facade.

1998
