MAR 24

81.24° N · 14.50° E
Svalbard Archipelago

The Blue Hour Storm

Winds peaked at 95km/h last night. We're waiting for the specific light that only happens during the 40 minutes of 'Blue Hour' here. The sensors are holding up at -32°C, but the batteries are a constant battle. Amara is tracking a polar bear family 2km north. Frame rates: 120fps. Format: 8K RAW.

MAR 19

27.98° N · 86.92° E
Mount Everest (Base Camp)

Oxygen at 5,300m

Logistics update: We've successfully moved 400kg of cinema gear to Camp 1. The custom DJI rigs are performing better than expected in the thin air, though flight times are cut by 40%. The goal is to capture the summit attempt of the brand ambassadors next Tuesday. The sheer scale of the Khumbu Icefall remains humbling.

MAR 12

3.16° S · 59.98° W
Amazon Basin, Brazil

Into the Green Canopy

98% humidity. The biggest challenge isn't the terrain, it's the mold. We're using humidity-controlled cases for all lenses. Layla has spent 14 hours in a tree blind waiting for a specific jaguar interaction. The biodiversity here is overwhelming, but the light under the canopy is notoriously difficult to master.

Technical Logistics

Engineering for
Zero Failure

When you're 800 miles from the nearest technician, your gear cannot fail. We use custom-modified systems built for the edge.

Cryo-Cinema Rigs

Our RED V-RAPTOR systems are fitted with internal heating elements and vacuum-sealed optics to prevent fogging in sudden temperature shifts.

High-Altitude Drones

Proprietary heavy-lift FPV drones with variable-pitch propellers, specifically tuned for the ultra-thin air of the 'Death Zone' (above 8,000m).

Abyssal Imaging

Titanium-housed cinema cameras rated to 300m. Integrated high-CRI lighting systems that replicate natural spectrums in pure darkness.

Field Sat-Link

Starlink-integrated production tents allowing for 4K dailies transmission from any point on Earth, ensuring seamless brand collaboration.
