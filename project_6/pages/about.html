The Collective Ethos

Built for the
Impossible

AFPC is more than a production house. We are a global response team of 24 specialists who operate where standard crews cannot. We don't just film adventure; we live it.

The Specialist Mindset

Why We Go to
The Edge

"Comfort is the enemy of the extraordinary." This is the core belief that drives every member of the Collective. When we are cold, tired, and operating at the limit of our physical endurance, that's when the most honest images are born.

Technical mastery of a camera is just 10% of what we do. The other 90% is survival, logistics, and the psychological resilience to wait for 'the moment' in conditions that make most people turn back. We don't bring the studio to the field; we make the field our studio.

Zero Staging

If it didn't happen naturally, it doesn't enter our lens. We prize truth over convenience.

Total Autonomy

Our teams are self-sufficient. We carry our own life support, power, and data systems.

What We Stand For

Our Core
Values

01

Authenticity

We never stage or fabricate. Every image captures an actual moment in an actual place, however long we wait for it.

02

Safety First

No shot is worth a life. We have full-time expedition safety officers and follow military-grade risk assessment protocols on every project.

03

Artistry

Technical excellence is baseline. What separates our work is compositional vision, narrative intuition, and the patience to wait for the extraordinary.

04

Sustainability

Carbon-neutral since 2021. We work with local guides, leave no trace, and donate 2% of revenue to wilderness conservation.

The People

The 24
Specialists

Marcus Veld

Co-Founder · Expedition Director

Former mountain guide. 17 years in extreme environments. Led 48 summit expeditions across 6 ranges. Everest North Ridge · Solo Antarctica traverse · K2 base camp winters.

Amara Cole

Co-Founder · Director of Photography

BAFTA-nominated DP. Pioneer of cinema at altitude. Courses taught at AFI and NFTS. Published in National Geographic, TIME, and The Guardian.

Jin Nakamura

Aerial & Drone Specialist

FAA Part 107 & EASA A2 certified. DJI Master stunt pilot. Has operated drones from 100m below sea level to 5,800m above it. Red Bull Air Race veteran.

Layla Osman

Lead Stills Photographer

National Geographic contributor since 2016. Published in 34 countries. World Press Photo finalist 2023. Specialises in long-exposure and low-light field photography.

Ericka Strand

Post-Production Supervisor

15 years in high-end commercial color grading. Trained at Technicolor Paris. Brings the field's raw capture to screen-ready cinematic delivery for broadcast and digital.

Dev Patel

Production Logistics Specialist

Ex-military logistics. Has coordinated gear into 42 countries, including restricted wilderness and conflict zones. No environment is unreachable.

Elena Rossi

Expedition Medic & Safety

WEM (Wilderness Expedition Medicine) certified. Specialized in high-altitude trauma and cold-weather survival. Keeps the crew alive so they can keep filming.

Kavi Singh

Marine & Rigging Specialist

Master commercial diver and expert rigger. Specialized in building camera platforms on vertical faces and underwater housings for heavy cinema gear.
